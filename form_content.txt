Case 1
Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Yousuf Ali Akon and others
Respondent: BIWTA and others

Facts: Petitioners were appointed as Master Pilots on probation with an express promise in the BIWTA advertisement that they would be permanently absorbed after satisfactory completion of one year probation. BIWTA failed to absorb them despite satisfactory service.

Statutes cited: Service regulations; legitimate expectation doctrine

============================================================

Case 2
Petitioner/Appellant: Sonali Bank
Respondent: Md. Abu Baker Sarker

Facts: Artha Rin suit where the borrower sought court intervention to exempt him from paying high interest on a bank loan. Court examined whether Artha Rin courts had power to exempt defendants from interest liability under the Artha Rin Adalat Ain.

Statutes cited: Artha Rin Adalat Ain 2003 Section 50

============================================================

Case 3
Petitioner/Appellant: Farhana Akhter Liza and others
Respondent: The Islamic University and others

Facts: Petitioners challenged disciplinary action taken by Islamic University against them. The authority failed to follow due process — it did not inform them of the charges and proposed punishment at the initial stage, and did not afford them an opportunity to file a reply before taking action against them.

Statutes cited: Constitution of Bangladesh Article 102(2); principles of due process and natural justice

============================================================

Case 4
Petitioner/Appellant: Bangladesh and others
Respondent: Sk. Md. Abdullah Faruque and others

Facts: High Court Division directed absorption of nationalized college teachers as Lecturers in government colleges relying on the 2018 Absorption Rules, although the writ petitioners had not prayed for absorption under those rules and the Rule Nisi had not been issued in that specific term. Appellate Division held the HCD travelled beyond the scope of the Rule Nisi.

Statutes cited: Constitution Article 102; Nationalized College Teachers Absorption Rules 2018; Supreme Court HCD Rules 1973 Chapter XIA

============================================================

Case 5
Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Badaruddin being dead his heirs
Respondent: Md. Shahidullah Miah

Facts: Plaintiff sought specific performance of a Bainapatra contract for sale of property. Defendant argued time in arbitration proceedings should extend the limitation period. Court held equity aids the vigilant and specific performance requires prompt filing.

Statutes cited: Limitation Act 1908 Article 113; Specific Relief Act

============================================================

Case 6
Petitioner/Appellant: Ain-O-Salish Kendra (ASK) and others
Respondent: Government of Bangladesh and others

Facts: ASK filed public interest litigation challenging eviction notices issued to slum residents on government land, arguing violation of fundamental rights. Government contended the land was allotted for government office construction and petitioners were unauthorized occupants without legal right to shelter.

Statutes cited: Government and Local Authority Lands and Buildings (Recovery of Possession) Ordinance 1970 Section 5(1); Constitution Articles 27, 31, 32

============================================================

Case 7
Petitioner/Appellant: Shoel Textile Mills Ltd and others
Respondent: Bangladesh and others

Facts: Petitioner had their loan rescheduled by the bank. After rescheduling, the bank or another authority attempted to treat the petitioner as a loan defaulter. Court examined Section 5 GaGa of the Bank Companies Act 1991 which defines loan defaulter.

Statutes cited: Bank Companies Act 1991 Sections 5 GaGa and 27 KaKa

============================================================

Case 8
Petitioner/Appellant: Kapasia Overseas Ltd
Respondent: Government of Bangladesh, Ministry of Expatriates Welfare and others

Facts: Petitioner's emigration recruiting license was cancelled along with forfeiture of securities. The authority issued the cancellation order without giving the petitioner any opportunity of being heard before the cancellation, in violation of Section 14(1) of the Emigration Ordinance 1982 which mandates a hearing before cancellation.

Statutes cited: Emigration Ordinance 1982 Section 14

Case 9
Petitioner/Appellant: Agrocorp International Pte Ltd
Respondent: Vietnam Northern Food Corporation

Facts: International commodity trading company sought appointment of an arbitrator from the respondent Vietnamese state corporation for formation of an arbitration tribunal. The respondent denied the existence of any arbitration agreement. Parties communicated through a broker whose status was disputed. Court examined whether email communications created contractual obligations.

Statutes cited: Arbitration Act 2001 Sections 9, 12, 17

Case 10
Petitioner/Appellant: Renuka Rani Mondol
Respondent: Biswajit Mondol and another

Facts: Appellate court found a registered gift deed was not acted upon because there was no evidence of delivery of possession to the donee. High Court found this reasoning incorrect and examined the legal validity of a registered Hindu gift deed without delivery of possession.

Statutes cited: Transfer of Property Act 1882 Section 123; Hindu gift law principles

Case 11
Petitioner/Appellant: Softesule Private Limited
Respondent: Government of Bangladesh and others

Facts: Petitioner challenged Review Panel decision in a procurement tender case where the Panel exceeded its jurisdiction under PPR Rule 60 and the authority refused to issue a No Objection Certificate despite the petitioner winning the tender.

Statutes cited: Public Procurement Rules 2008 Rule 60; CPTU jurisdiction

Case 12
Petitioner/Appellant: Jamir Ali and others
Respondent: Bangladesh and another

Facts: Plaintiffs sought declaration of title over land lost to river erosion in 1967 that reappeared in 1974. Government argued land vested in the Government under Section 86 of the State Acquisition and Tenancy Act as amended in 1972 since it reappeared after the cut-off date.

Statutes cited: State Acquisition and Tenancy Act 1950 Section 86; P.O. No. 135 of 1972

============================================================

Case 13
Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Reza Kamal
Respondent: Secretary, Ministry of Civil Aviation and others

Facts: Biman Bangladesh Airlines employee challenged a promotion order on the ground that Biman's promotion process relied solely on interview performance without considering Annual Confidential Reports accumulated over years of service. This created scope for arbitrariness and unlawful decisions in promotion.

Statutes cited: Bangladesh Biman promotion rules; principles of fair promotion procedure

============================================================

Case 14
Petitioner/Appellant: Sonali Bank
Respondent: Md. Abu Baker Sarker

Facts: Sonali Bank challenged a lower court order that purported to exempt the defendant from liability of paying interest on a loan. The bank argued that only the financial institution itself has the exclusive right to exempt any person from payment of interest, not the court.

Statutes cited: Artha Rin Adalat Ain 2003 Section 50

============================================================

Case 15
Petitioner/Appellant: Ramesh Chandra Das and others
Respondent: Sureshwar Mazumdar and others

Facts: Tenants in suit properties denied the title of the plaintiffs as landlords. Plaintiffs sought eviction but defendants argued that notice under Section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act was required. Court examined whether denial of landlord's title forfeits the tenant's right to notice before eviction.

Statutes cited: Transfer of Property Act Section 106; landlord-tenant relationship; denial of title

============================================================

Case 16
Petitioner/Appellant: Probir Kumar Dey alias Saiful and another
Respondent: Shipra Rani Dey and others

Facts: Partition suit over 4.81 decimals of land after owner's death. Hindu widow claimed same share as son under the Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act 1937. One son converted to Islam before suit was filed. Court examined rights of widow, converted son, and unmarried daughter to inheritance and partition.

Statutes cited: Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act 1937 Section 3; Caste Disability Removal Act 1850; Dayabhaga school of Hindu Law

============================================================

Case 17
Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Giasuddin
Respondent: Government of Bangladesh, Ministry of Primary and Mass Education and others

Facts: Nationalized primary school teachers challenged rules under the 2013 Service Conditions Rules that reduced their effective service years by 50% and created discriminatory seniority provisions favouring direct recruits over nationalized teachers, affecting their promotions and time scales.

Statutes cited: অধিগ্রহণকৃত বেসরকারি প্রাথমিক বিশ্ববিদ্যালয়ের শিক্ষক (চাকুরি শর্তাবলী নির্ধারণ) বিধিমালা ২০১৩ Rules 2(Ga) and 9(1)

============================================================

Case 18
Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Sirajuddwla
Respondent: State and another

Facts: Petitioner faced both criminal proceedings under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act and execution proceedings in the Artha Rin Adalat over the same loan transaction. This created a situation where the petitioner would have to deposit 50% under both proceedings, together almost covering the full claimed amount, leading to potential unjust enrichment of the creditor.

Statutes cited: Negotiable Instruments Act 1881 Section 138; Artha Rin Adalat Ain 2003 Section 41; Code of Criminal Procedure Section 344

============================================================

Case 19
Petitioner/Appellant: Shetu International Pvt. Ltd and others
Respondent: Artha Rin Adalat-2, Dhaka and another

Facts: In an Artha Rin proceeding, the plaintiff-bank published summons in a newspaper at its own instance without following proper procedure — no summons was served through Process Server or by post, and the court had passed no order authorizing newspaper publication. The plaintiff acted on its own motion without court authorization.

Statutes cited: Artha Rin Adalat Ain 2003 Sections 6(2) and 7(1)

============================================================

Respondent: Mohammad Enamur Rashid Chowdhury

Facts: Plaintiff sought permanent injunction over property where defendant claimed title based on City Corporation tax receipts without a legitimate chain of title. Court identified defendant's claim as that of a land grabber whose deeds did not refer to any specific immovable property.

Petitioner/Appellant: Md. Nurul Islam and others
Respondent: Charge Officer and Appeal Officer and others

Facts: Petitioners challenged a Settlement Officer who exercised power under Rule 42 to nullify land record proceedings up to the Appellate Division level, reversing the entire course of the parent law's prescribed sequence. Government had not delegated power to reverse parent law proceedings.

Statutes cited: State Acquisition and Tenancy Rules 1955 Rule 42; Settlement Officer jurisdiction

Respondent: Secretary, WR, MoWD and others

Facts: A Steno-Typist was promoted to Stenographer and claimed entitlement to a fresh slot of time scales after promotion. The question was whether benefits drawn as Steno-Typist prevented entitlement to time scales in the promoted post of Stenographer.

Case 25
Petitioner/Appellant: Mainul Hossain and another
Respondent: Bangladesh and others

Facts: Petitioners challenged cancellation of their land lease where the authority cancelled the lease first and then called them to appear with valid papers afterward. The principle of natural justice required hearing before decision, not after.

Statutes cited: Principles of natural justice; audi alteram partem
